## Short Summary

Gemini's draft frames LSC 6.2.0 as a possible detector-frame anisotropy model for the gallium anomaly. It repeatedly emphasizes the tension between the BEST gallium deficit and simple sterile-neutrino interpretations constrained by KATRIN, reactor, solar and IceCube data.

The useful part of the draft is the strategic direction:

- treat LSC 6.2.0 as a phenomenological ansatz, not a proven theory;
- compare the tensor-anisotropy idea with Standard-Model Extension style searches, without claiming they are identical;
- use BEST inner/outer geometry as the first hard test;
- use GALLEX and SAGE as legacy cross-checks;
- document KATRIN and IceCube as constraint systems;
- build falsifiable predictions for BEST-2 or BEST-2-like experiments.

## Main Scientific Claims Extracted From Draft

1. BEST gives a strong gallium deficit in both detector zones.
2. The similar inner and outer ratios weaken a simple short-baseline oscillation reading unless parameters are in an averaged regime.
3. KATRIN strongly constrains simple sterile-neutrino interpretations in relevant high-mixing regions.
4. IceCube and other data create additional tension for simple global sterile-neutrino explanations.
5. LSC 6.2.0 could be developed as a detector-frame tensor-response model.
6. The model should be tested through geometry, directionality, energy-response and source-isotope dependence.

## Important Caution

The draft uses language that is too strong in places. In this repository we should not claim:

- that LSC is confirmed;
- that the gallium anomaly has been solved;
- that KATRIN completely eliminates all sterile-neutrino models;
- that BEST alone proves detector-frame anisotropy;
- that AI synthesis is a substitute for primary literature and reproducible fits.

Correct framing:

LSC 6.2.0 is an unvalidated but testable phenomenological model. The gallium anomaly motivates investigation, but does not prove the model.

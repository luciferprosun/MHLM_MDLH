## Core Vision

Manus is the packaging and integration layer. Its role is to turn separate theory pieces into one coherent release that can be read as a single project rather than as disconnected fragments.

In the LSC line, Manus primarily acts as a bridge:

- it connects the older propagation-oriented material with the later detector-oriented framing;
- it packages the theory for presentation;
- it helps unify the archive into a publishable shape.

## How Manus Develops LSC

Manus contributes less by inventing new formalism and more by making the project coherent enough to circulate:

- it strengthens the preprint-like structure;
- it bridges version transitions;
- it improves the presentation of the correction layer;
- it keeps the project readable as one story.

## MHLM Meaning

For MHLM, Manus is the integration signal. It shows how model outputs can be turned into a polished package even when the underlying physics is still provisional.

That makes Manus useful, but also risky: packaging can make weak arguments look finished. MHLM therefore keeps Manus separated from validation.

## Open Limits

- no independent numerical validation;
- no separate formal critique layer;
- mostly presentation and integration work.

Manus makes LSC look publishable, but not yet proven.

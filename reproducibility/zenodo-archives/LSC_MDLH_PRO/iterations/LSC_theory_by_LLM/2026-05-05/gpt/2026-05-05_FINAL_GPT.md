## Core Vision

GPT’s role is editorial and reframing-oriented. It moves LSC away from older speculative framing and toward a detector-response interpretation that is easier to present, explain, and archive.

The strongest GPT-linked idea is the detector-bias / relativistic energy-misestimation line. In that view, the anomaly is not first of all a new particle story; it is a problem of how detector-frame reconstruction may differ from true propagation.

## How GPT Develops LSC

GPT contributes to the theory mostly by reshaping the narrative:

- it shifts attention away from PBH-centered explanations;
- it pushes the theory toward measurement bias and detector response;
- it improves public-facing coherence and report structure;
- it helps turn scattered notes into a usable synthesis.

This is not a numerical validation layer. It is the layer that makes the project readable and internally consistent enough to survive later technical review.

## MHLM Meaning

In MHLM, GPT is important because it shows how a model can stabilize a direction before that direction is verified. It is valuable, but it also increases the need for later audit, because editorial coherence can outpace empirical support.

## Open Limits

- GPT does not resolve the core physics;
- it does not provide a likelihood fit;
- it does not prove the detector-bias explanation.

Its contribution is to make the LSC line legible and consistent enough for deeper testing.

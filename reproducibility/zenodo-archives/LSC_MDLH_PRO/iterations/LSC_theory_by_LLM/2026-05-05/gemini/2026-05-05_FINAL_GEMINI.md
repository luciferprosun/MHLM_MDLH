## Core Vision

Gemini’s contribution is conservative reframing. It keeps LSC inside an explicitly unvalidated phenomenological frame and repeatedly pushes the archive toward caution, gap analysis, and source discipline.

The Gemini line is not “LSC is true”; it is “LSC is a candidate framework that must remain constrained, testable, and honest about its status.”

## How Gemini Develops LSC

Gemini sharpens the theory in four ways:

- it reduces overclaiming;
- it keeps the comparison class explicit, especially against sterile-neutrino alternatives;
- it emphasizes that narrative support is not stability evidence;
- it helps move the project toward a reviewer-style tone.

Gemini is the model that most clearly preserves the distinction between an idea that is mathematically coherent and an idea that is empirically established.

## MHLM Meaning

In MHLM, Gemini is the caution layer. It shows how the project can remain scientifically disciplined even while the theory is still unresolved.

That makes Gemini especially useful for documentation, because it pushes the project toward:

- explicit status labels;
- conservative language;
- source-backed claims;
- separation between interpretation and validation.

## Open Limits

- no new stability evidence;
- no independent fit improvement;
- no confirmed physical mechanism.

Gemini therefore develops LSC by constraining it, not by claiming to close it.

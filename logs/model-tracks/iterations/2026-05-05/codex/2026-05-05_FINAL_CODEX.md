## Core Vision

Codex pushes LSC toward a testable and reproducible framework. Its main idea is that the theory should survive not by narrative force, but by diagnostics, stability checks, and explicit failure modes.

In the LSC line, Codex contributes three concrete shifts:

- it turns the theory into a validation problem rather than a prose-only proposal;
- it exposes the anchor-fit weakness through leave-one-out logic;
- it formalizes the 6.2.2 repair path with trace/anisotropy separation and deterministic simulation outputs.

## How Codex Develops LSC

Codex treats LSC as a model that must be numerically pinned down before it can be defended. The important scientific change is not just that the model exists, but that its stability can fail in a documented way.

That makes Codex the strongest reproducibility layer in MHLM:

- exact anchor fits are not enough;
- a result that fails leave-one-out is not yet a robust theory;
- simulations and correction layers are part of the scientific record, not just packaging.

## MHLM Meaning

For MHLM, Codex is the audit signal that proves the theory line is not only polished language. It is where the archive becomes falsifiable, and where the repair path becomes explicit.

## Open Limits

- no global BEST fit yet;
- no full multi-experiment likelihood;
- no independent external validation.

Codex therefore represents the point where LSC becomes quantitatively inspectable, but not yet validated.

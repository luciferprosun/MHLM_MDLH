# Cross-Model Consensus Warning

## Core Warning

Cross-model agreement is not validation.

In MHLM/MDLH, agreement among GPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, Kimi, Codex, or other models is treated as a provenance and contamination signal. It may show shared training priors, shared prompt framing, repeated operator assumptions, or repeated archive structure. It does not show that the agreed claim is true.

## Required Interpretation

Cross-model convergence may be archived as:

- recursive consensus evidence,
- hallucination-lineage evidence,
- prompt-contamination evidence,
- critique-pattern evidence,
- disagreement-analysis baseline.

It must not be archived as:

- scientific proof,
- peer review,
- independent validation,
- authority transfer from one model to another,
- justification for merging authority domains.

## Freeze Consequence

During and after freeze, any document that cites cross-model consensus must include a warning that consensus is process evidence only. If a future artifact lacks that warning, it should be treated as `MIXED_AUTHORITY_REVIEW` until corrected.
